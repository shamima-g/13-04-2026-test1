## Test Design: Authentication, Role Configuration, and Role-Based Routing

### Story Summary

**Epic:** 1 â€” Authentication and Role-Based Routing
**Story:** 1 of 1
**As a** signed-in user, **I want to** be automatically sent to the right page for my role when I sign in, **so that** I can immediately see and work on what is relevant to me without manual navigation.

---

### Business Behaviors Identified

- After successful sign-in, the app reads the user's role and redirects them to the correct landing page automatically
- `/` is purely a redirect hub â€” not a real content page
- Any route under `/tasks` or `/tasks/all` requires an active session; visiting without one redirects to sign-in
- `/tasks/all` is admin-only; a team-member visiting it is silently sent to `/tasks` with no explanation
- Exactly two valid roles: `admin` and `team-member`
- Sign-in page is keyboard accessible

---

### Key Decisions Surfaced by AI

1. **Sign-in page ownership:** The story references the sign-in page but doesn't specify whether this story builds/modifies it or reuses an existing template. AC-11 (keyboard accessibility) implies at minimum it must be verified.
2. **Team-member hitting `/tasks/all` â€” server vs client redirect:** "Silent" redirect behaviour depends on whether it's server-side (URL replaced, no history trace) or client-side (adds history entry). Next.js `redirect()` produces server-side behaviour.
3. **Unrecognised role value:** If a session has a role other than `admin` or `team-member`, the redirect logic has no defined target. Likely a non-production edge case but worth noting.

---

| 4 | Signed-in admin visits `/` | Active session, role: `admin` | Redirected to `/tasks/all` |
| 5 | Unauthenticated user visits `/` | No session | Redirected to sign-in page |
| 6 | Unauthenticated user visits `/tasks` | No session | Redirected to sign-in page |
| 7 | Unauthenticated user visits `/tasks/all` | No session | Redirected to sign-in page |

**Edge cases:**
- Invalid credentials â†’ user stays on sign-in page with error message

### BA Decisions Required

**Decision 1 â€” Scenario 8 (team-member hitting `/tasks/all`):**
Should the redirect be completely silent with no browser history trace, or is standard server-side redirect behaviour (URL replaced, no user-visible message) acceptable?
- **Option A:** Fully silent, no browser history trace, no message whatsoever
- **Option B:** Server-side redirect â€” URL is replaced (no `/tasks/all` in history), no message shown *(this is what Next.js `redirect()` produces)*

**Decision 2 â€” Edge 2 (session expiry):**
After re-authenticating, should the app return the user to where they were, or always send them to their role-based landing page?
- **Option A:** Always redirect to role-based landing page (simpler, consistent with AC-1/AC-2)
- **Option B:** Preserve the originally requested URL and redirect there after re-authentication (common UX pattern, adds complexity)
